# GMDSS — OOW Exam Focus

GMDSS covers global maritime distress and safety communications under SOLAS IV. Examiners test sea area equipment requirements, distress procedures, and the functional requirements of the system.

KEY EXAM AREAS:
- Four sea areas — A1 (VHF, ~20-30nm), A2 (MF, ~150nm), A3 (Inmarsat, ~70N-70S), A4 (polar, HF)
- Nine functional requirements — ship-to-shore, shore-to-ship, ship-to-ship distress alerting, SAR coordination, locating, MSI, general comms, bridge-to-bridge
- Distress procedure — DSC alert on Ch.70 (VHF) or 2187.5 kHz (MF), followed by MAYDAY voice on Ch.16
- EPIRB — 406 MHz, HRU at 1.5-4m depth, registration mandatory, 48-hour minimum battery
- NAVTEX (518 kHz, 490 kHz) and SafetyNET for maritime safety information broadcasts

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Confusing the four sea areas and which equipment is required in each
- Not knowing the DSC alert must precede the voice MAYDAY call
- Forgetting EPIRB registration requirements and HRU servicing intervals
- Mixing up urgency (PAN PAN) and safety (SECURITE) message priorities
- Not knowing that SART shows 12 dots on radar and operates on 9 GHz (X-band only)
