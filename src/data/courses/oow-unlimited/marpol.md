# MARPOL (International Convention for the Prevention of Pollution from Ships) — OOW Unlimited

## Overview

MARPOL 73/78 is the principal international convention covering the prevention of pollution of the marine environment by ships from operational or accidental causes. For the OOW Unlimited candidate, thorough knowledge of MARPOL is essential — particularly the discharge criteria for each annex, the record-keeping requirements, and the special area provisions. The MCA oral examination will test the candidate's understanding of their responsibilities as the watchkeeping officer in preventing pollution, knowing when discharges are permitted, and what to do in the event of an accidental pollution incident.

## Key Regulations / Standards

- **MARPOL 73/78** (International Convention for the Prevention of Pollution from Ships, 1973, as modified by the Protocol of 1978)
- **Annex I** — Prevention of Pollution by Oil (mandatory)
- **Annex II** — Control of Pollution by Noxious Liquid Substances in Bulk (mandatory)
- **Annex III** — Prevention of Pollution by Harmful Substances Carried by Sea in Packaged Form (mandatory)
- **Annex IV** — Prevention of Pollution by Sewage from Ships (mandatory)
- **Annex V** — Prevention of Pollution by Garbage from Ships (mandatory)
- **Annex VI** — Prevention of Air Pollution from Ships (mandatory)
- **SOPEP** — Shipboard Oil Pollution Emergency Plan (required under Annex I)
- **Garbage Management Plan** — Required under Annex V
- **MCA MGN 564** — MARPOL Annex VI: Air Pollution

## Core Concepts

### Annex I — Oil Pollution Prevention

Annex I is the most commonly examined. Key discharge criteria for machinery space bilge water outside Special Areas:

- The ship is proceeding en route
- Oil content of the effluent does not exceed 15 parts per million (ppm)
- The ship has in operation oil filtering equipment (OWS — oily water separator) with 15 ppm alarm and automatic stopping device
- The discharge is not mixed with oil cargo residues or chemicals
- Not within a Special Area

**Special Areas for Annex I**: Mediterranean Sea, Baltic Sea, Black Sea, Red Sea, Gulf area, Gulf of Aden, Antarctic area, North West European waters, Oman area of the Arabian Sea, Southern South African waters. In these areas, the same criteria apply (15 ppm), but the key point is that oil tanker cargo residue discharges are prohibited.

**Oil tankers** (cargo area discharges outside Special Areas): Additional requirements including being more than 50 nautical miles from the nearest land, instantaneous rate of discharge not exceeding 30 litres per nautical mile, and total quantity discharged not exceeding 1/30,000 of the total cargo.

**Oil Record Book (ORB)**: Part I for machinery space operations (all ships of 400 GT and above), Part II for cargo/ballast operations (oil tankers of 150 GT and above). Must record all transfers, discharges, ballasting, and tank cleaning operations. Entries signed by the officer in charge and countersigned by the Master.

**SOPEP**: Required on all ships of 400 GT and above. Contains procedures for dealing with oil spills, reporting requirements, contacts (coastal state, flag state, P&I club, salvage), and equipment list.

### Annex II — Noxious Liquid Substances in Bulk

- Substances categorised as X, Y, Z, or OS (Other Substances):
  - **Category X**: Major hazard — discharge prohibited, must be discharged to reception facilities.
  - **Category Y**: Hazard — limited discharge permitted after tank washing with residues to reception facility.
  - **Category Z**: Minor hazard — less stringent discharge criteria.
  - **OS**: No harm — no MARPOL requirements.
- Tank washing procedures, prewash requirements, and Procedures and Arrangements (P&A) Manual must be followed.
- Cargo Record Book required.

### Annex IV — Sewage

- **Treated sewage**: May be discharged at any distance using an approved sewage treatment plant meeting IMO standards.
- **Untreated sewage**: May be discharged at a distance greater than 12 nautical miles from the nearest land, at a moderate rate, while the ship is proceeding en route at not less than 4 knots.
- **Comminuted and disinfected sewage**: May be discharged at a distance greater than 3 nautical miles from the nearest land.
- **Special Areas (Baltic Sea)**: Stricter standards — passenger ships must use approved treatment plants meeting nitrogen and phosphorus removal standards.

### Annex V — Garbage

The most restrictive annex. General rule: **discharge of all garbage into the sea is prohibited**, with limited exceptions:

- **Food waste** (comminuted to <25mm): Outside Special Areas, more than 3 nm from land. Inside Special Areas, more than 12 nm from land.
- **Cargo residues not harmful to the marine environment**: Outside Special Areas, more than 12 nm from land.
- **Cleaning agents and additives in wash water**: Outside Special Areas, discharge permitted.
- **All plastics**: Discharge ALWAYS prohibited, everywhere.
- **Garbage Record Book**: Required on ships of 400 GT and above, or certified to carry 15 or more persons. Records of all garbage discharges, incineration, and deliveries to reception facilities. Retained for 2 years.
- **Garbage Management Plan**: Required on all ships of 100 GT and above.

### Annex VI — Air Pollution

- **SOx Emission Control Areas (SECA/ECA)**: Sulphur content of fuel oil must not exceed 0.10% m/m within ECAs (Baltic Sea, North Sea, North American ECA, US Caribbean ECA).
- **Global sulphur cap**: 0.50% m/m since 1 January 2020 (outside ECAs).
- **NOx emissions**: Tier I, II, and III standards based on engine build date. Tier III applies within NOx ECAs.
- **Ozone-depleting substances**: Deliberate emissions prohibited. Regulations on halon, CFC, and HCFC systems.
- **Shipboard incineration**: Permitted using approved incinerators. Certain materials (PCBs, MARPOL Annex I/II/III residues containing heavy metals, PVC) must NOT be incinerated onboard.
- **Energy Efficiency**: EEDI (Energy Efficiency Design Index) for new ships, SEEMP (Ship Energy Efficiency Management Plan) for all ships, and CII (Carbon Intensity Indicator) rating annually.

## Common Exam Questions

1. What are the discharge criteria for oily bilge water outside a Special Area?
2. Your vessel has an oil spill on deck. What are your immediate actions and reporting requirements?
3. What can and cannot be discharged under Annex V inside a Special Area?
4. What is the current global sulphur cap and what are the ECA limits?
5. What entries are required in the Oil Record Book?
6. Explain the SOPEP and your responsibilities under it.
7. What are the discharge criteria for sewage?

## Key Points to Remember

- **15 ppm** is the maximum oil content for machinery space bilge discharge — this number will come up repeatedly.
- **All plastics — never discharged.** No exceptions, no Special Area distinctions. This is the simplest rule to remember and the one most commonly tested.
- **Oil Record Book entries must be made at the time of the operation**, not retrospectively. Falsifying the ORB is a serious criminal offence in many jurisdictions.
- **SOPEP reporting**: Under MARPOL Protocol I, any discharge or probable discharge of oil must be reported to the nearest coastal state without delay.
- **Garbage Record Book retention**: 2 years after the last entry. ORB retention: 3 years after the last entry.
- **Fuel changeover**: When entering an ECA, the vessel must change to compliant fuel. The changeover procedure and timing should be recorded, and the fuel system must be flushed of high-sulphur fuel before entering the ECA.

## Examiner Focus Areas

Examiners frequently test Annex I (oil discharge criteria, ORB, SOPEP) and Annex V (garbage discharge rules, particularly the distinction between Special Areas and non-Special Areas). Candidates should be able to state discharge criteria precisely, not approximately. Annex VI has become increasingly important with the global sulphur cap and ECA requirements — expect questions on fuel changeover procedures and compliance. The examiner will also look for an understanding of the OOW's personal responsibility in preventing pollution: if the candidate is on watch and a crew member attempts an illegal discharge, the OOW must stop it and report it.
