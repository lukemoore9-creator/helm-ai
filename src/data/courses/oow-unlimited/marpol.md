# MARPOL — OOW Exam Focus

MARPOL 73/78 covers prevention of pollution from ships across six annexes. Examiners test discharge criteria, special area restrictions, and record-keeping requirements for each annex.

KEY EXAM AREAS:
- Annex I (oil) — 15 ppm limit, OWS operation, Oil Record Book, SOPEP requirements for 400 GT+
- Annex IV (sewage) — treated (anywhere), untreated (>12nm, en route, 4+ knots), comminuted (>3nm)
- Annex V (garbage) — all discharge prohibited except limited food waste outside Special Areas; plastics NEVER
- Annex VI (air) — global sulphur 0.50%, ECA 0.10%, NOx tiers, EEDI/SEEMP/CII requirements
- Special Areas — stricter discharge rules apply; know which annexes have them and key areas (Med, Baltic, North Sea)

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Confusing discharge criteria between annexes — mixing up oil, sewage, and garbage distance rules
- Not knowing which annexes have Special Areas (I, II, IV, V, VI) and the stricter requirements
- Forgetting that Annex V default is prohibition — discharge is the exception, not the rule
- Not mentioning the Oil Record Book when discussing Annex I obligations
- Confusing SOPEP (shipboard oil pollution emergency plan) with garbage management plans
