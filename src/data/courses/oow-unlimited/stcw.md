# STCW — OOW Exam Focus

STCW sets standards for training, certification, and watchkeeping including rest hours and competency requirements. Examiners focus on practical watchkeeping duties and hours of rest compliance.

KEY EXAM AREAS:
- OOW competencies under STCW II/1 — navigation, cargo handling, ship operation, radio communications
- Hours of rest — minimum 10 hours in any 24-hour period (may be split into max two periods, one must be 6+ hours), 77 hours in any 7-day period
- Watchkeeping principles — OOW is Master's representative, never leave bridge unattended, fitness to keep watch
- Master call procedures — when OOW must call the Master (visibility, traffic, weather, doubt)
- Certificate revalidation — 5-year validity, sea service and approved training requirements

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Getting the rest hour numbers wrong — 10/24 and 77/7 with the split rules
- Not knowing when the OOW must call the Master and trying to list situations instead of the principle (whenever in doubt)
- Forgetting that the OOW cannot hand over the watch if the relieving officer is unfit
- Not mentioning alcohol limits (0.08% BAC or 0.05% in some flag states)
- Confusing STCW with ISM Code — STCW is crew competency, ISM is safety management systems
