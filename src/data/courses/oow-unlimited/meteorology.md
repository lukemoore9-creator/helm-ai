# Meteorology — OOW Exam Focus

Meteorology covers weather systems, interpretation, and their impact on voyage planning. Examiners focus on practical decision-making — identifying systems, predicting changes, and knowing avoidance actions.

KEY EXAM AREAS:
- Frontal systems — cloud sequences, wind shifts, and pressure changes for warm, cold, and occluded fronts
- Tropical revolving storms — formation conditions, dangerous vs navigable semicircle, avoidance rules
- Buys Ballot's Law — practical application to determine storm centre bearing
- Fog types — advection (warm air over cold sea) vs radiation (land, overnight) and when to expect each
- Weather routing — using synoptic charts, barometric trends, and swell patterns for passage decisions

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Confusing wind veering (clockwise shift, cold front passage) with backing (anticlockwise)
- Not knowing the 12 points rule for TRS dangerous semicircle avoidance in Northern Hemisphere
- Describing fog types without linking to practical situations where each occurs
- Forgetting that significant wave height is the average of the highest one-third of waves
- Not mentioning barometric pressure trend as the primary early TRS warning indicator
