# Navigation — OOW Exam Focus

Navigation covers passage planning, position fixing, chart work, tides, and compass errors. Examiners want to see a structured four-stage approach and practical understanding of errors and limitations.

KEY EXAM AREAS:
- Four-stage passage planning (appraisal, planning, execution, monitoring) per SOLAS V
- Position fixing methods — visual bearings, radar ranges, GPS, and cross-checking between them
- Under-keel clearance calculation including squat, swell, and heel allowances
- ECDIS operation — safety contour vs safety depth, anti-grounding alarms, over-scaling risks
- Tidal calculations — secondary port adjustments, tidal stream interpolation

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Not mentioning all four stages of passage planning when asked
- Relying solely on GPS without discussing cross-checking or degradation modes
- Forgetting squat effect in shallow water UKC calculations
- Confusing variation (chart-based) with deviation (vessel-specific) in compass errors
- Not knowing how to apply deviation from a deviation card
