# Stability — OOW Exam Focus

Stability covers ship stability principles, calculations, and the IS Code 2008 criteria. Examiners expect you to explain concepts clearly and perform basic calculations from hydrostatic data.

KEY EXAM AREAS:
- GM (metacentric height) — relationship between KG, KM, and what positive/negative GM means practically
- GZ curve — reading righting levers, angle of vanishing stability, and IS Code 2008 minimum criteria
- Free surface effect — why it reduces GM, virtual rise of G, how slack tanks affect stability
- Inclining experiment — purpose (finding lightship KG), procedure, and when it must be repeated
- Dangerous conditions — angle of loll, cargo shift, icing, and how to identify/respond to each

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Confusing stiff (large GM, short roll period) with tender (small GM, long roll period)
- Not knowing IS Code criteria values (area under GZ curve, minimum GM of 0.15m, GZ at 30 degrees)
- Explaining free surface effect without mentioning it is the tank breadth that matters most
- Forgetting that angle of loll requires adding weight LOW, not moving weight to the high side
- Mixing up trim calculations — MCTC method and the effect of moving weights fore/aft
