# SOLAS (International Convention for the Safety of Life at Sea) — OOW Unlimited

## Overview

SOLAS is the most important international treaty governing the safety of merchant ships. First adopted in 1914 in response to the Titanic disaster, the current version (SOLAS 1974, as amended) sets minimum standards for the construction, equipment, and operation of ships. For an OOW Unlimited candidate, a thorough understanding of SOLAS is essential — it underpins almost every other topic in the oral examination. The MCA examiner will expect candidates to reference specific SOLAS chapters and regulations when discussing safety equipment, navigation, fire protection, and emergency procedures.

## Key Regulations / Standards

- **SOLAS 1974** as amended, with Protocol of 1988
- **Chapter I** — General Provisions (application, surveys, certificates)
- **Chapter II-1** — Construction: subdivision, stability, machinery and electrical installations
- **Chapter II-2** — Fire protection, fire detection and fire extinction
- **Chapter III** — Life-saving appliances and arrangements
- **Chapter IV** — Radiocommunications (GMDSS)
- **Chapter V** — Safety of Navigation
- **Chapter VI** — Carriage of Cargoes
- **Chapter VII** — Carriage of Dangerous Goods
- **Chapter IX** — Management for the Safe Operation of Ships (ISM Code)
- **Chapter X** — Safety Measures for High-Speed Craft
- **Chapter XI-1** — Special Measures to Enhance Maritime Safety
- **Chapter XI-2** — Special Measures to Enhance Maritime Security (ISPS Code)
- **Chapter XII** — Additional Safety Measures for Bulk Carriers
- **Chapter XIV** — Safety Measures for Ships Operating in Polar Waters (Polar Code)

## Core Concepts

### Chapter I — General Provisions

- **Application**: SOLAS applies to ships engaged on international voyages (with specific tonnage thresholds). Warships, fishing vessels, and pleasure craft are excluded.
- **Surveys and certificates**: Initial survey before the ship enters service, periodic surveys (renewal every 5 years), intermediate surveys, annual surveys, and additional surveys after repair or modification.
- **Key certificates**: Cargo Ship Safety Construction Certificate, Cargo Ship Safety Equipment Certificate, Cargo Ship Safety Radio Certificate, or the harmonised Cargo Ship Safety Certificate. Passenger Ship Safety Certificate.
- **Port State Control (PSC)**: SOLAS Regulation I/19 allows port states to inspect foreign-flagged vessels to verify certificates are valid and the ship's condition matches. Deficiencies can lead to detention.

### Chapter II-1 — Construction and Stability

- Subdivision and damage stability requirements, particularly for passenger vessels (probabilistic damage stability assessment).
- Watertight integrity: watertight bulkheads, doors, and their operation and testing.
- Bilge pumping arrangements and requirements.
- Intact stability criteria (linked to the IS Code — International Code on Intact Stability).

### Chapter II-2 — Fire Protection

- Structural fire protection: A-class divisions (steel or equivalent, insulated to prevent passage of smoke and flame and limit temperature rise for 60 minutes), B-class divisions (non-combustible material preventing passage of flame for 30 minutes).
- Main vertical fire zones: maximum 40m in length.
- Fire detection and alarm systems, fixed fire-extinguishing systems, fire-fighting equipment.
- Means of escape: two routes from every space, emergency escape breathing devices (EEBDs).

### Chapter III — Life-Saving Appliances

- Requirements for lifeboats, liferafts, rescue boats, lifebuoys, lifejackets, immersion suits, and thermal protective aids.
- Regulation III/19: Training, drills, and exercises — abandon ship drill and fire drill within 24 hours of departure, weekly musters, monthly equipment checks.
- LSA Code provides detailed specifications.

### Chapter V — Safety of Navigation

This is the most relevant chapter for the OOW and covers:

- **Regulation V/5**: Meteorological services and warnings.
- **Regulation V/10**: Ships' routeing — Traffic Separation Schemes.
- **Regulation V/14**: Ship manning — adequate and qualified crew.
- **Regulation V/19**: Carriage requirements for shipborne navigational systems and equipment (ECDIS mandatory for ships built after 1 July 2012, with retrofit dates for existing ships).
- **Regulation V/19.2**: Equipment list — gyro compass, magnetic compass, radar (3cm and 10cm), AIS, ECDIS, echo sounder, speed and distance measuring device, rudder angle indicator, rate of turn indicator (for ships over 50,000 GT), BNWAS.
- **Regulation V/28**: Records of navigational activities — the deck log.
- **Regulation V/31**: Danger messages — obligation to communicate dangers to navigation.
- **Regulation V/33**: Distress messages — obligations.
- **Regulation V/34**: Safe navigation and avoidance of dangerous situations.

### Chapter IX — ISM Code

- The International Safety Management Code requires companies to establish a Safety Management System (SMS).
- The Designated Person Ashore (DPA) provides the link between company and ship.
- Document of Compliance (DOC) for the company, Safety Management Certificate (SMC) for the ship.

### Chapter XI-2 — ISPS Code

- Three security levels: Level 1 (normal), Level 2 (heightened), Level 3 (exceptional).
- Ship Security Plan (SSP), Ship Security Officer (SSO), Company Security Officer (CSO), Port Facility Security Officer (PFSO).
- Ship Security Alert System (SSAS) — covert distress alerting.
- International Ship Security Certificate (ISSC).

## Common Exam Questions

1. What surveys are required under SOLAS and at what intervals?
2. What navigational equipment is required under SOLAS V/19 for your vessel?
3. Explain the difference between A-class and B-class fire divisions.
4. What are the requirements for abandon ship drills under SOLAS III/19?
5. What is the ISPS Code and how does it apply to your role as OOW?
6. Describe the role of Port State Control and what happens during an inspection.
7. What are the carriage requirements for ECDIS?

## Key Points to Remember

- **SOLAS applies to vessels on international voyages** — not all vessels. Know the exceptions.
- **Certificates have validity periods**: Most safety certificates are valid for 5 years with annual and intermediate surveys. Allow them to lapse and the vessel can be detained.
- **The Cargo Ship Safety Equipment Certificate** covers LSA and fire-fighting equipment — this is what PSC often scrutinises.
- **SOLAS V/34**: The Master shall not be constrained by the shipowner, charterer, or any other person from taking any decision which, in the professional judgement of the Master, is necessary for safe navigation.
- **ECDIS carriage requirements** are now fully phased in for all SOLAS vessels.
- **ISPS Level changes**: Only the Contracting Government sets the security level, not the shipowner or Master (though the Master can implement higher security measures).

## Examiner Focus Areas

Examiners use SOLAS as a framework for asking about many topics. They may ask "under which SOLAS regulation..." to test whether the candidate can locate requirements within the convention. Candidates should be comfortable referencing chapter and regulation numbers. The examiner will particularly focus on Chapter V (navigational equipment and practices), Chapter III (LSA drills and requirements), and Chapter IX (ISM Code application). Understanding how SOLAS interacts with other conventions (STCW, MARPOL, COLREGS) demonstrates the breadth of knowledge expected at OOW level.
