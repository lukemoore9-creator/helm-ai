# Safety — OOW Exam Focus

Safety covers emergency procedures, fire-fighting, life-saving appliances, and man overboard recovery. Examiners expect structured responses showing you can take command of any emergency.

KEY EXAM AREAS:
- Fire triangle, fire classes (A-F), and fixed extinguishing systems (CO2, foam, water spray)
- Engine room fire procedure — boundary cooling, ventilation shutdown, release sequence
- LSA requirements — lifeboat capacity, launching procedure, HRU operation
- MOB recovery turns — Williamson (delayed response), Anderson (immediate), Scharnow (high speed)
- Muster list requirements and abandon ship procedure sequence

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Not mentioning boundary cooling before releasing CO2 in engine room fires
- Confusing lifeboat launching sequence steps or forgetting on-load release gear checks
- Describing MOB turns without stating when each is most appropriate
- Forgetting EPIRB and SART deployment in abandon ship procedures
- Not knowing HRU activation depth (1.5-4m) and servicing interval (annual)
