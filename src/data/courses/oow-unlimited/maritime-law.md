# Maritime Law — OOW Exam Focus

Maritime law covers the legal framework including UNCLOS, flag state duties, port state control, and liability conventions. Examiners test practical knowledge of jurisdiction, Master's authority, and legal responsibilities.

KEY EXAM AREAS:
- UNCLOS zones — territorial sea (12nm), contiguous zone (24nm), EEZ (200nm), high seas, and rights in each
- Flag state vs port state responsibilities — registration, certification, surveys vs PSC inspections and detention
- Master's authority — SOLAS V/34 means no one can override the Master on safety decisions
- ISM Code — SMS elements, DPA role, DOC (company certificate) vs SMC (ship certificate)
- Collision — duty to stand by, assist, exchange particulars; proportional liability

COMMON MISTAKES:
- Confusing UNCLOS zones and the rights that apply in each (especially EEZ vs territorial sea)
- Not knowing what triggers a PSC detention and the difference between a deficiency and a detention
- Mixing up DOC (Document of Compliance, held by company) and SMC (Safety Management Certificate, held by ship)
- Forgetting Master's overriding authority when discussing ISM or company instructions
- Not knowing the basics of salvage (LOF, no cure no pay) or pollution liability (CLC/Bunkers conventions)
